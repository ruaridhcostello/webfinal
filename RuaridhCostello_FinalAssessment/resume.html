     During my second semester at Seneca college, I began the course WEB222. I did not originally think I would enjoy the course as much as I did, but around the second day of class I quickly realized that web design was something that I would want to develop more as a skill after the course was over. I thoroughly enjoyed my professor’s teaching style and I found that the fact that classes were being done online made the whole learning process even more enjoyable. It was very easy to follow along with what the professor was doing and with the lectures having been recorded, I liked that I was able to look back upon past classes in the event that I had forgotten or misunderstood something that was taught. I also enjoyed learning JavaScript, as it felt familiar to me due to the similarities between JavaScript and C language which I had learned the previous semester. I found it very interesting learning how CSS worked and the different ways it could be implemented to help change the appearance of a web page. I’m very excited to learn more CSS in the future, as I am heavily considering going into a career path focusing on SQL Databases and CSS is also used in the design and management of them. I plan on taking the skills I have learned in both this WEB222 course and my DBS211 course and improving upon them both through Seneca courses, and during my own free time.
